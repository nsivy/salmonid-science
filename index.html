Exploring Salmonid Ecology & Conservation

About Me

I'm Nathan Ivy, a researcher specializing in aquatic toxicology and salmonid ecology. My work focuses on the real-time impacts of stormwater toxicants on juvenile coho salmon, including 6PPD-quinone toxicity and temperature-mediated toxicity. I am passionate about habitat restoration and applied conservation science.

Current Research

Miller Creek Stormwater Study

Investigating real-time stormwater toxicity on juvenile coho salmon using dynamic exposure experiments.

6PPD-Quinone Toxicity

Examining the lethal and sublethal effects of tire-derived chemicals on salmonid populations.

Temperature-Mediated Toxicity

Exploring how temperature fluctuations influence toxicant uptake and mortality in freshwater systems.
